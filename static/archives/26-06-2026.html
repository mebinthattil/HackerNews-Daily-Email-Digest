An entire Herculaneum scroll has been read for the first time

1098 points • by verditelabs • 44 comments
Summary
PHerc. 1667, a carbonized Herculaneum papyrus sealed since 79 AD, was fully read without physical opening by high‑resolution phase‑contrast X‑ray micro‑tomography (BM18 beamline, ESRF) followed by 3‑D reconstruction, surface flattening, and machine‑learning ink detection. The resulting digital sheet reveals a Stoic ethical treatise dated to the 2nd century BC, covering about twenty‑two columns of Greek text, with several continuous passages recovered despite fragmentary loss. A second scroll, PHerc. Paris 4, was imaged at higher resolution, making ink directly visible in the 3‑D X‑ray data and confirming the 2023 Grand Prize reading. A third scroll, PHerc. 139, yielded its title and author: *Philodemus, On Gods, Book 8*. All tomographic data, reconstructed surfaces, transcriptions, and processing code are released under a Creative Commons licence (scrollprize.org, GitHub), enabling independent verification and scalable application to the hundreds of remaining Herculaneum rolls. The project exemplifies open, collaborative science driven by the Vesuvius Challenge community.
